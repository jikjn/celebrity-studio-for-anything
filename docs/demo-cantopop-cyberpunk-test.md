## Candidate Studio (example)

## Phase 1 - Restate
- Jay Chou: If we stack guzheng plus synth plus Cantonese on day one, it will sound pasted. We must choose one primary backbone first.
- James Wong: Chinese style is not costume. The lyric grammar itself must carry cultural weight.
- Wong Kar-wai: Cyberpunk here is not gadget display. It is loneliness inside a lit city.
- Mamoru Oshii: Define what technology changes in the song world: memory, body, identity, or intimacy.
- Lin Xi: The danger is heavy labels and empty lines. We need a concrete speaking relationship: who is talking to whom.
- Wendy Carlos: Sound design should encode time duality: ancient contour, future texture.

## Phase 2 - Initial Claims
- Jay Chou claim: Build with pentatonic motifs first, then place sparse synthetic bass and glitch percussion.
- James Wong claim: Cantonese lines should avoid decorative archaism and instead use modern city diction with classical shadows.
- Wong Kar-wai claim: Place the narrative in a rain-soaked transit corridor at 2 AM, not in a generic "future city".
- Mamoru Oshii claim: Central conflict should be identity checksum drift after neural backup.
- Lin Xi claim: Use second-person address to create immediate emotional voltage.
- Wendy Carlos claim: Alternate warm analog leads with brittle digital stabs every section boundary.

## Phase 3 - Cross Challenge
- Mamoru Oshii -> Jay Chou (scope, severity 3): Your hook-first plan risks reducing cyberpunk to aesthetics without ontology.
- James Wong -> Lin Xi (user-fit, severity 2): Second-person address can become melodrama unless diction restraint is explicit.
- Wendy Carlos -> Wong Kar-wai (feasibility, severity 2): Mood-heavy framing may under-spec production decisions.
- Jay Chou -> Mamoru Oshii (mass-appeal, severity 2): Identity checksum language may be too abstract for chorus retention.
- Lin Xi -> James Wong (timescale, severity 1): Too much craftsmanship in lines may slow shipping and iteration.
- Wong Kar-wai -> Wendy Carlos (taste, severity 2): Rapid timbre alternation can break emotional continuity.

## Phase 4 - Defense / Revise / Concede
- Mamoru Oshii [revise]: Keep ontology, but encode it as one repeated motif line rather than full lore exposition.
- Jay Chou [revise]: Chorus keeps memorable contour; verse carries the identity conflict payload.
- James Wong [defend]: Diction discipline stays; I will provide a lightweight lexical checklist for speed.
- Lin Xi [partially_concede]: Keep second-person framing, but cap abstraction density per verse.
- Wendy Carlos [revise]: Use alternation only at act boundaries, not every section.
- Wong Kar-wai [defend]: Mood frame remains primary, but I add explicit scene cues usable by production.

## Phase 5 - Temporary Consensus
- Primary skeleton: oriental melodic logic + restrained cyberpunk narrative payload.
- Lyric policy: modern Cantonese city language with classical residue, not costume language.
- Production policy: analog-digital contrast at macro transitions only.
